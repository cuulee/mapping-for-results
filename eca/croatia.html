CROATIA

Croatia has made tremendous progress over the last twenty years and this hard work has been rewarded by successful completion of accession negotiations with the European Union, with EU membership expected in July 2013. However, the crisis has taken a severe toll on Croatia, with the economy contracting by 7.2 percent in 2009-2010. The 2011 growth is projected at below 1 percent. Croatia needs continue its efforts to unlock the country’s growth potential. Structural reforms are needed in almost every sector to ensure fiscal sustainability, while at the same time better targeting is needed to improve the efficiency of fiscal expenditures. The World Bank has been focusing its assistance on helping Croatia prepare for successful EU integration and implement structural reforms which would boost competitiveness and mitigate the impact of the crisis. The Bank is also helping with investments in major trading routes such as the Port of Rijeka; promoting research and development, aligning the Croatian agriculture sector to that of the EU and supporting the judicial and revenue administration reforms. It has also assisted with the revitalization of war-affected areas through job creation and mine clearing, improvements in the quality of lives of vulnerable groups, and the protection of the environment and promotion of energy efficient solutions. All of these efforts have the aim of helping Croatia enter the EU with the foundations of a strong and stable economy.

Active financed activities as of September 21, 2011.
