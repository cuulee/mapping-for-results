UGANDA

Uganda has established a strong record of prudent macroeconomic management and structural reform over the past two decades. Growth remained robust even in the face of consecutive exogenous shocks. However, due to rapid population growth, real GDP growth per capita averaged only 3.4 percent in the 1990s and around four percent in the 2000s. The World Bank’s lending program in Uganda is one of the largest in Africa and is projected to grow if the country maintains its growth and development focus. The World Bank provides direct budget support and commitments for infrastructure, education and social development.
